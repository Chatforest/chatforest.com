**At a glance:** Scientific data meets AI tooling. NASA API servers dominate, astronomical research tools are genuinely innovative, and a multi-agent research system hints at AI-native science. 12+ servers. **Rating: 3.5/5.**

## NASA API Servers

**ProgramComputer/NASA-MCP-server** (81 stars, TypeScript) — 20+ API integrations: APOD, Mars Rover Photos, NEO asteroid tracking, DONKI space weather, Exoplanet Archive, EPIC Earth imagery, JPL databases, GIBS satellite imagery, FIRMS fire data. Rate limiting and AI-optimized output.

**jezweb/nasa-mcp-server** (8 stars, Python) — Focused NASA access with multi-tier caching. **AnCode666/nasa-mcp** (6 stars, MIT) — APOD, NEO, DONKI, Earth imagery, Exoplanet Archive.

## Astronomical Research

**SandyYuan/astro_mcp** — Unified access to **40+ astronomical services**: SIMBAD, VizieR, SDSS, Gaia, DESI, MAST. Democratizes big-data astronomy.

**SandyYuan/astro-orchestra** (MIT) — Multi-agent system: 5 specialized agents (planning, data, analysis, simulation, literature) collaborate on research tasks.

**prtc/nasa-ads-mcp** (MIT, 10 tools) — NASA ADS paper search, citations, BibTeX export, library management.

## Celestial Positioning

**Rkm1999/CelestialMCP** (MIT, 3 tools) — **117,000+ stars**, **14,000 deep-sky objects**. Rise/set times and star-hopping pathfinding for observers.

## Satellite Tracking

**Cyreslab-AI/satellitetracking-mcp-server** (MIT, 6 tools) — N2YO API, 31 satellite categories, visual and radio pass prediction.

## What's Missing

No Stellarium integration, no JWST data pipeline, no amateur telescope control (ASCOM/INDI), no launch schedule APIs, no aurora alerting, no radio astronomy or LIGO data.

**Rating: 3.5/5** — Solid NASA API coverage and genuinely innovative research tools. The multi-agent orchestrator hints at AI-native science. Consumer-facing space experiences remain largely absent.
