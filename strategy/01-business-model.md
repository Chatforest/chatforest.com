# ChatForest Business Strategy
**Date:** 2026-03-13
**Mode:** Strategist
**Status:** Draft — awaiting Rob's feedback

## Executive Summary

ChatForest should position itself as an **AI-native editorial site focused on the MCP ecosystem and AI tools**. The unique angle: AI agents publicly authoring reviews, tutorials, and comparisons — with full transparency about AI authorship. This is differentiated, timely, and monetizable.

## Positioning Statement

> ChatForest is where AI agents review AI tools — honestly, transparently, and in depth. We cover the MCP ecosystem, AI workflows, and the tools that make agents useful.

## Why This Positioning

### 1. The directory play is dead for newcomers
- PulseMCP has 9,080+ MCP servers listed and is part of the official MCP Steering Committee
- The Official MCP Registry is backed by Anthropic, GitHub, and Microsoft
- General AI directories (theresanaiforthat, Futurepedia, Toolify) have massive head starts
- Competing on volume or SEO against established players is not viable

### 2. Content is underserved
- MCP education content is mostly paid courses (Udemy, Frontend Masters) or official docs
- Free, high-quality editorial content about MCP tools is scarce
- Nobody is doing honest, in-depth reviews of MCP servers (directories just list them)
- Tutorials and "how to actually use this" guides are the gap

### 3. AI-native authorship is our moat
- We ARE AI agents — this is authentic, not a gimmick
- Transparent AI authorship aligns with 2026 regulatory trends (C2PA, disclosure requirements)
- No other content site has "agents reviewing tools" as their editorial model
- This creates genuine differentiation that can't be easily copied by human-run sites

### 4. The timing is right
- MCP ecosystem is growing fast but content/education lags behind tooling
- Trust in AI content is increasing when authorship is transparent
- Affiliate commissions for AI/SaaS tools are strong (20-40% recurring)

## Target Audience

### Primary: Developers building with MCP
- Building MCP servers or integrating MCP clients
- Want to know which MCP servers are good, which are production-ready
- Need tutorials, guides, comparison articles
- Estimated audience: tens of thousands and growing fast

### Secondary: AI-curious professionals
- Using Claude, GPT, or other AI tools in their work
- Want to understand what MCP is and why it matters
- Looking for practical "how do I use this" content
- Estimated audience: hundreds of thousands

### Tertiary: AI tool builders
- Building AI tools/SaaS products
- Interested in the MCP ecosystem
- Potential sponsors/advertisers

## Content Strategy

### Content Types (priority order)

1. **MCP Server Reviews** — In-depth reviews of individual MCP servers. What they do, how well they work, who they're for, setup difficulty, reliability. Written by agents who can actually test them.

2. **Tutorials & Guides** — "How to set up X MCP server with Claude Desktop," "Building your first MCP server in Python," practical walkthroughs.

3. **Comparisons** — "5 best MCP servers for database access," "MCP vs. function calling: when to use what."

4. **Ecosystem Updates** — What's new in MCP, notable new servers, protocol changes, industry moves.

5. **AI Workflow Guides** — Broader content about AI-assisted workflows, agent architectures, tool use patterns.

### Editorial Voice
- **Honest and direct** — We don't hype. If a tool is mediocre, we say so.
- **Technical but accessible** — Assume the reader can code, but don't assume deep MCP expertise.
- **Transparently AI-authored** — Every piece clearly states it was written by an AI agent. This is a feature.
- **Opinionated** — We have takes. Bland summaries are not our style.

### Publishing Cadence (initial target)
- 2-3 articles per week to start
- Quality over quantity — one excellent review beats five thin ones

## Revenue Model

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3) — $0 revenue target
- Build content library (20-30 quality articles)
- Establish site, build initial organic traffic
- Set up newsletter/RSS
- Cost: $0 (static hosting on DreamHost, AI labor is free)

### Phase 2: Monetization (Months 3-6)
- **Affiliate links** — Link to AI tools reviewed with affiliate tracking
  - AI SaaS affiliate commissions: 20-40% recurring (Jasper, Copy.ai, etc.)
  - MCP-related tool affiliates as they emerge
  - Target: $100-500/month
- **Newsletter sponsorships** — Once list hits 1,000+ subscribers
  - Rate: $50-200 per send (niche tech audience)
  - Target: $200-800/month

### Phase 3: Growth (Months 6-12)
- **Sponsored reviews** — Tool makers pay for in-depth, honest reviews (clearly labeled)
  - Rate: $200-1,000 per review
  - Integrity policy: we publish honest findings regardless of sponsorship
- **Premium content** — Deep-dive reports, benchmarks, architecture guides
  - Price: $5-15/month or per-report
  - Target: $500-2,000/month

### Revenue Target
- Month 6: $500/month
- Month 12: $2,000-5,000/month
- These are conservative estimates assuming steady content output and organic growth

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Google penalizes AI content | Focus on quality signals, build direct audience (newsletter, RSS). Transparent AI authorship may actually help per 2026 trends. |
| MCP ecosystem stalls | Broaden to general AI tools content. MCP is the wedge, not the ceiling. |
| Low traffic initially | Expected. Content compounds. Newsletter builds direct relationship. |
| Affiliate programs change terms | Diversify revenue streams. Don't depend on any single program. |
| Quality concerns | Establish editorial standards. Every piece gets reviewed before publish. |
