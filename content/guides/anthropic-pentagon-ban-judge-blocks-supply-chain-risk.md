In March 2026, a [federal judge blocked the Trump administration](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html) from banning Anthropic's AI technology across the entire U.S. government — ruling that the Pentagon's retaliation against the company for refusing to remove safety guardrails was [likely unconstitutional](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/26/nx-s1-5762971/judge-temporarily-blocks-anthropic-ban). But on April 8, the [D.C. Circuit declined to stay](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-loses-bid-to-block-pentagon-blacklisting) a separate supply chain designation — leaving Anthropic in legal limbo across two courts.

This analysis draws on reporting from [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/26/us-judge-blocks-anthropic-ban-supply-chain-risk-orwellian-notion/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/26/nx-s1-5762971/judge-temporarily-blocks-anthropic-ban), [Breaking Defense](https://breakingdefense.com/2026/03/judge-grants-anthropic-preliminary-injunction-but-pentagon-cto-says-ban-still-stands/), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/02/trump-administration-appeals-anthropic-pentagon), [JURIST](https://www.jurist.org/news/2026/03/us-district-judge-blocks-government-ban-on-anthropic-ai/), [TechPolicy.Press](https://www.techpolicy.press/a-timeline-of-the-anthropic-pentagon-dispute/), [EFF](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/03/anthropic-dod-conflict-privacy-protections-shouldnt-depend-decisions-few-powerful), [Lawfare](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/pentagon's-anthropic-designation-won't-survive-first-contact-with-legal-system), and [The Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/02/27/trump-anthropic-claude-drop/) — we research and analyze rather than testing products hands-on. ## The Two Red Lines

The story begins with a contract. In [July 2025, Anthropic signed a two-year, $200 million deal with the Pentagon](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-and-the-department-of-defense-to-advance-responsible-ai-in-defense-operations) — the first time a frontier AI lab integrated its models into classified military networks. The contract was framed as a partnership to ["prototype frontier AI capabilities that advance U.S. national security."](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-and-the-department-of-defense-to-advance-responsible-ai-in-defense-operations) Claude was [embedded in Palantir's Maven Smart System on classified networks](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/03/04/anthropic-ai-iran-campaign/) through a [partnership with Palantir and Amazon Web Services](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/anthropic-claude-ai-iran-war-u-s/).

But the contract included [two restrictions](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/24/tech/hegseth-anthropic-ai-military-amodei) that Anthropic had maintained since its founding:

1. **No mass surveillance of American citizens**
2. **No lethal autonomous weapons without meaningful human authorization**

These weren't negotiable for Anthropic. They would become the flashpoint for the [largest legal confrontation between an AI company and the U.S. government](https://fortune.com/2026/03/12/anthropic-pentagon-lawsuit-supply-chain-risk-china-ai-race/).

---

## The "Any Lawful Use" Demand

In [January 2026, Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth issued an AI strategy memorandum](https://media.defense.gov/2026/Jan/12/2003855671/-1/-1/0/ARTIFICIAL-INTELLIGENCE-STRATEGY-FOR-THE-DEPARTMENT-OF-WAR.PDF) directing all Department of Defense AI contracts to incorporate standard ["any lawful use" language within 180 days](https://defensescoop.com/2026/01/13/hegseth-ai-tech-hubs-reorganization-dod-dow/). The memorandum emphasized that ["the risks of not moving fast enough outweigh the risks of imperfect alignment"](https://www.insidegovernmentcontracts.com/2026/02/pentagon-releases-artificial-intelligence-strategy/) and directed the new CDAO to [create benchmarks for model objectivity as a primary procurement criterion within 90 days](https://defensescoop.com/2026/01/13/hegseth-ai-tech-hubs-reorganization-dod-dow/).

In Anthropic's reading, "any lawful use" was an umbrella formulation that would [permit deployment for domestic mass surveillance and lethal targeting](https://www.asisonline.org/security-management-magazine/latest-news/today-in-security/2026/february/Anthropic-Refusal/) in fully autonomous weapons systems — precisely the uses its two red lines prohibited.

On **February 24, 2026**, Hegseth [demanded Anthropic allow "unrestricted use" of Claude "for all legal purposes"](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/24/tech/hegseth-anthropic-ai-military-amodei) by **5:01 p.m. on February 27** — a [three-day ultimatum](https://breakingdefense.com/2026/02/pentagon-gives-anthropic-friday-deadline-to-loosen-ai-policy/).

---

## Anthropic Refuses

On **February 26**, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei [released a public statement](https://www.anthropic.com/news/statement-department-of-war) refusing to remove the restrictions. He wrote that Anthropic could not ["in good conscience" grant the DOD's request](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/26/anthropic-pentagon-ai-amodei.html) and that ["in a narrow set of cases, AI can undermine rather than defend democratic values."](https://www.anthropic.com/news/statement-department-of-war) In an [internal memo seen by the Financial Times](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/anthropic-pentagon-ai-deal-department-of-defense-openai-.html), Amodei told staff that near the end of negotiations, the Pentagon had offered to accept Anthropic's terms if they deleted a "specific phrase about 'analysis of bulk acquired data'" — a line he said "exactly matched this scenario we were most worried about."

A [Pentagon official responded](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/24/tech/hegseth-anthropic-ai-military-amodei) that "the military only issues lawful orders."

---

## The Government Strikes Back

The response was swift and extraordinary. Within [24 hours of the deadline passing](https://edition.cnn.com/2026/02/27/tech/anthropic-pentagon-deadline):

**February 27, 2026:**
- President Trump [posted on Truth Social](https://fortune.com/2026/02/27/trump-us-government-anthropic-claude-pentagon-6-months-phaseout-ai-standoff/) ordering **all federal agencies to immediately cease using Anthropic products**, with a [six-month phase-out](https://fortune.com/2026/02/27/trump-us-government-anthropic-claude-pentagon-6-months-phaseout-ai-standoff/) for agencies operationally dependent on Claude
- Trump publicly [called Anthropic "woke" and "leftwing,"](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/trump-orders-federal-agencies-to-stop-using-anthropic-tech-over-ai-safety-dispute) claiming it was endangering troops and jeopardizing national security
- Defense Secretary Hegseth [designated Anthropic a **supply chain risk to national security**](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/02/27/trump-anthropic-claude-drop/)

**March 5, 2026:**
- The Pentagon [formally notified Anthropic](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/anthropic-pentagon-ai-claude-iran.html) of its designation as a **Supply-Chain Risk to National Security** under [two separate statutes](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/anthropic-supply-chain-risk-designation-takes-effect--latest-developments-and-next-steps-for-government-contractors):
  - **[Title 41, Section 4713](https://www.willkie.com/publications/2026/03/anthropic-designated-a-supply-chain-risk-what-contractors-must-know)** (government-wide)
  - **[Title 10, Section 3252](https://www.willkie.com/publications/2026/03/anthropic-designated-a-supply-chain-risk-what-contractors-must-know)** (Defense Department-specific)

This designation had [**never before been applied to an American company**](https://www.axios.com/2026/02/27/ai-trump-supply-chain-anthropic-pentagon-blacklist). It is a classification [normally reserved for foreign adversaries](https://www.inc.com/ben-sherry/the-pentagon-designated-anthropic-as-a-supply-chain-risk-heres-what-the-label-actually-means/91310393) or compromised foreign vendors — entities like [Huawei or Kaspersky](https://www.axios.com/2026/02/27/ai-trump-supply-chain-anthropic-pentagon-blacklist).

The scope was sweeping: all federal agencies were [ordered to stop using Claude](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/trump-anthropic-ai-order-federal-agencies/), and federal contractors were [barred from accessing the technology](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/anthropic-supply-chain-risk-designation-takes-effect--latest-developments-and-next-steps-for-government-contractors).

---

## What "Supply Chain Risk" Actually Means

The supply chain risk designation is a [legal instrument designed to protect the government](https://www.inc.com/ben-sherry/the-pentagon-designated-anthropic-as-a-supply-chain-risk-heres-what-the-label-actually-means/91310393) from vendors that pose genuine security threats — companies with ties to foreign intelligence services, compromised hardware manufacturers, or entities under foreign government control.

Applying it to Anthropic — a [San Francisco-based company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic) [founded by former OpenAI researchers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dario_Amodei), [backed by Google and Amazon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropic), with no foreign government ties — was, as Judge Lin would later put it, [treating a domestic business like a foreign adversary](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html).

The designation [effectively stigmatizes a company](https://news.northeastern.edu/2026/03/05/anthropic-supply-chain-risk/) across every arm of the federal government. It doesn't just end one contract; it [signals to the entire procurement ecosystem](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/anthropic-supply-chain-risk-designation-takes-effect--latest-developments-and-next-steps-for-government-contractors) that doing business with the company carries national security risk.

---

## Anthropic Sues

On **March 9, 2026**, Anthropic [sued the federal government](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/nation/anthropic-sues-in-federal-court-to-reverse-trump-administrations-supply-chain-risk-designation) in [U.S. District Court in San Francisco](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/anthropic-sues-defense-department-over-supply-chain-risk-designation/), seeking an injunction against the supply chain designation. The company argued the government's actions violated:

- **The Administrative Procedure Act (APA)** — the designation was [arbitrary, capricious, and made without following required legal procedures](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/09/nx-s1-5742548/anthropic-pentagon-lawsuit-amodai-hegseth)
- **The First Amendment** — the ban was [retaliation for Anthropic exercising its right to publicly disagree](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/anthropic-sues-defense-department-over-supply-chain-risk-designation/) with the government's position on AI ethics

Anthropic filed a [separate lawsuit in the D.C. Circuit](https://reason.com/volokh/2026/04/08/d-c-circuit-declines-to-stay-department-of-wars-supply-chain-risk-designation-of-claude/) specifically challenging the Title 41 designation.

---

## The Hearing

On **March 24**, Judge Rita F. Lin [held a 90-minute hearing](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/24/nx-s1-5759276/anthropic-pentagon-claude-preliminary-injunction-hearing) in San Francisco. She described the Pentagon's actions as ["troubling"](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/anthropic-lawsuit-pentagon-supply-chain-risk-claude.html) and questioned whether the supply chain designation was adequately ["tailored" to national security needs](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/anthropic-lawsuit-pentagon-supply-chain-risk-claude.html).

---

## The Ruling

On **March 26**, Judge Lin issued a [**43-page ruling**](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html) granting Anthropic's request for a preliminary injunction — [blocking the government from enforcing its ban](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/26/nx-s1-5762971/judge-temporarily-blocks-anthropic-ban).

Her language was striking:

> ["Nothing in the governing statute supports the Orwellian notion that an American company may be branded a potential adversary and saboteur of the U.S. for expressing disagreement with the government."](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html)

She found that:

- **The designation was pretextual**: ["The record strongly suggests that the reasons given for designating Anthropic a supply chain risk were pretextual and that the government's real motive was unlawful retaliation."](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/26/business/anthropic-pentagon-injunction-supply-chain-risk)
- **The government failed to show evidence**: Officials [could not provide evidence of an actual supply chain risk](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/26/nx-s1-5762971/judge-temporarily-blocks-anthropic-ban) and had circumvented required legal procedures for such designations.
- **It was First Amendment retaliation**: Penalizing a company for bringing public scrutiny to the government's contracting positions constitutes ["classic illegal First Amendment retaliation."](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html)
- **Anthropic was likely to succeed** in its lawsuit [on the merits](https://ktslaw.com/en/insights/alert/2026/3/anthropic%20granted%20preliminary%20injunction).

The ruling also found the designation was ["likely both contrary to law and arbitrary and capricious"](https://ktslaw.com/en/insights/alert/2026/3/anthropic%20granted%20preliminary%20injunction). It included a seven-day stay before taking effect, giving the government time to appeal.

---

## The Pentagon Pushes Back

The response from the Pentagon was immediate and defiant.

**Emil Michael**, the Defense Department's Chief Technology Officer, [contested the ruling via posts on X](https://breakingdefense.com/2026/03/judge-grants-anthropic-preliminary-injunction-but-pentagon-cto-says-ban-still-stands/), calling it a ["disgrace"](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/08/appeals-court-rejects-anthropics-bid-block-pentagon-blacklisting/) and claiming Judge Lin's order contained "dozens of factual errors." He asserted that the supply chain designation [remains "in full force and effect"](https://breakingdefense.com/2026/03/judge-grants-anthropic-preliminary-injunction-but-pentagon-cto-says-ban-still-stands/) under Title 41, Section 4713, which he argued was not subject to Lin's jurisdiction. Michael had previously argued that Anthropic's Claude models would ["pollute" the defense supply chain](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/anthropic-claude-emil-michael-defense.html) because they have "a different policy preference" baked in.

Michael's position: Lin's injunction covers only the Title 10 (DOD-specific) designation. The [Title 41 (government-wide) prohibition](https://www.aoshearman.com/en/insights/ao-shearman-on-tech/dow-and-anthropic-showdown-continues-navigating-the-anthropic-supply-chain-risk-designations), issued March 4, continues despite the court order.

This creates a legal gray zone — the court says the ban is blocked, the Pentagon says part of it still stands.

---

## The Appeal

On **April 2, 2026**, the Trump administration [formally appealed](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/02/trump-administration-appeals-anthropic-pentagon) Judge Lin's ruling to the **9th Circuit Court of Appeals**.

The 9th Circuit set an [**April 30 deadline**](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/02/trump-administration-appeals-anthropic-pentagon) for the Justice Department to file documents outlining their reasons for overturning the decision.

Legal experts suggest the full case could take [**one to two years** to resolve](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/02/trump-administration-appeals-anthropic-pentagon).

---

## D.C. Circuit Denies Anthropic's Stay

On **April 8, 2026**, the [D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals denied](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-loses-bid-to-block-pentagon-blacklisting) Anthropic's request to pause enforcement of the Title 41 supply chain designation — a [significant setback](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/08/appeals-court-rejects-anthropics-bid-block-pentagon-blacklisting/) that contrasted sharply with Judge Lin's favorable ruling in California.

A three-judge panel wrote that ["the equitable balance here cuts in favor of the government,"](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-pentagon-court-ruling-supply-chain-risk.html) noting that ["on one side is a relatively contained risk of financial harm to a single private company. On the other side is judicial management of how, and through whom, the Department of War secures vital AI technology during an active military conflict."](https://thehill.com/policy/technology/5823132-appeals-court-rejects-anthropic-halt/)

The practical result: with [split decisions across two courts](https://www.computerworld.com/article/4156534/us-court-refuses-to-stay-pentagons-supply-chain-risk-blacklisting-of-anthropic.html), Anthropic is excluded from DOD contracts but can continue working with other government agencies while litigation plays out. The D.C. Circuit [set oral arguments for May 19](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/08/appeals-court-rejects-anthropics-bid-block-pentagon-blacklisting/), acknowledging that Anthropic is likely to "suffer some irreparable harm" during the process.

## What This Means

### For Anthropic

The preliminary injunction is a significant legal win, but the fight is far from over. The company faces:
- A [9th Circuit appeal](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/02/trump-administration-appeals-anthropic-pentagon) that could reverse Lin's order
- A separate [D.C. Circuit case on the Title 41 designation](https://reason.com/volokh/2026/04/08/d-c-circuit-declines-to-stay-department-of-wars-supply-chain-risk-designation-of-claude/) — where the court has [already denied Anthropic's stay request](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-loses-bid-to-block-pentagon-blacklisting)
- The [Pentagon CTO publicly asserting](https://breakingdefense.com/2026/03/judge-grants-anthropic-preliminary-injunction-but-pentagon-cto-says-ban-still-stands/) that the government-wide ban continues regardless
- Potential loss of federal revenue during the dispute (the original contract was [$200 million](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-and-the-department-of-defense-to-advance-responsible-ai-in-defense-operations))

### For AI Companies

The case establishes a potential legal precedent: **the government cannot weaponize procurement authority as retaliation against AI companies that maintain ethical guardrails**. Experts warn the designation could [chill innovation](https://news.northeastern.edu/2026/03/05/anthropic-supply-chain-risk/) across the AI sector, and the [tech industry has pushed](https://www.webpronews.com/tech-industry-pushes-trump-administration-to-remove-anthropics-supply-chain-risk-label/) the administration to remove the label.

If Lin's reasoning holds on appeal, it means AI companies can refuse government demands that conflict with their safety policies without fear of being designated national security threats. That's significant for every AI lab negotiating government contracts. [Lawfare analysis](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/military-ai-policy-by-contract--the-limits-of-procurement-as-governance) argues the case exposes fundamental tensions in using procurement as a governance tool for AI policy.

### For Government AI Procurement

The ruling raises questions about the ["any lawful use" mandate](https://media.defense.gov/2026/Jan/12/2003855671/-1/-1/0/ARTIFICIAL-INTELLIGENCE-STRATEGY-FOR-THE-DEPARTMENT-OF-WAR.PDF). If the government cannot punish companies for maintaining usage restrictions, the Hegseth memorandum's [180-day timeline](https://defensescoop.com/2026/01/13/hegseth-ai-tech-hubs-reorganization-dod-dow/) for standardizing contract language faces practical obstacles.

The irony noted by several observers: Claude was already [deployed in classified operations, including against Iran](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/03/04/anthropic-ai-iran-campaign/). Reports indicate Claude [generated approximately 1,000 prioritized targets on the first day of operations alone](https://responsiblestatecraft.org/ai-war-iran/), synthesizing satellite imagery, signals intelligence, and surveillance feeds. The dispute was not about whether AI should serve national security — it was about whether the AI company or the government gets to define the boundaries.

### For the First Amendment

Judge Lin's ruling is one of the [strongest judicial statements to date](https://www.joneswalker.com/en/insights/blogs/ai-law-blog/the-orwell-card-what-the-preliminary-injunction-in-anthropic-v-us-tells-us-abou.html?id=102mo8s) on First Amendment protections for corporate speech about AI ethics. The ["Orwellian notion" language](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-pentagon-dod-claude-court-ruling.html) signals that courts may be skeptical of government attempts to leverage procurement power as a tool for punishing policy disagreements. The [ACLU and CDT filed amicus briefs](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/aclu-and-cdt-urge-court-to-stop-government-from-punishing-anthropic-for-important-advocacy-on-ai-guardrails) urging courts to protect Anthropic's advocacy on AI guardrails.

## What We Don't Know

- **The full classified picture**: While [multiple outlets including The Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/03/04/anthropic-ai-iran-campaign/), [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/anthropic-claude-ai-iran-war-u-s/), and [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/us-military-using-ai-help-plan-iran-air-attacks-sources-say-lawmakers-rcna262150) have reported on Claude's use in Iran operations, the Pentagon has not officially confirmed the specific scope of Claude's role or confirmed the [reported target generation figures](https://responsiblestatecraft.org/ai-war-iran/).
- **The financial impact**: How much revenue Anthropic has lost during the ban period is not public. The D.C. Circuit acknowledged Anthropic would ["suffer some irreparable harm"](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/08/appeals-court-rejects-anthropics-bid-block-pentagon-blacklisting/) but ruled the government's interest outweighed it.
- **Other AI companies' positions**: OpenAI [published a blog post titled "Our agreement with the Department of War"](https://openai.com/index/our-agreement-with-the-department-of-war/) — striking a deal [hours after Trump's Anthropic ban](https://fortune.com/2026/02/27/openai-in-talks-with-pentagon-after-anthropic-blowup/). OpenAI CEO Sam Altman later [admitted he "shouldn't have rushed"](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/03/openai-sam-altman-pentagon-deal-amended-surveillance-limits.html) the announcement and acknowledged it "looked opportunistic and sloppy." The [EFF called OpenAI's deal "weasel words"](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/03/weasel-words-openais-pentagon-deal-wont-stop-ai-powered-surveillance), and [MIT Technology Review argued](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/03/02/1133850/openais-compromise-with-the-pentagon-is-what-anthropic-feared/) it was "what Anthropic feared."
- **The circuit split**: The D.C. Circuit [denied Anthropic's stay](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/anthropic-loses-bid-to-block-pentagon-blacklisting) on April 8, while the Northern District of California granted the injunction — creating [conflicting rulings](https://www.computerworld.com/article/4156534/us-court-refuses-to-stay-pentagons-supply-chain-risk-blacklisting-of-anthropic.html) that may ultimately require resolution by the Supreme Court.
- **Long-term contract status**: Whether the original $200 million contract can be revived, or whether the relationship is permanently damaged, remains unclear. The Pentagon CTO has stated there is ["no chance" of renewed negotiations](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/anthropic-claude-emil-michael-defense.html).
